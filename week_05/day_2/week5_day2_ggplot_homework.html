Q2: What is the distribution of backpack weights that students are carrying? Choose an appropriate graph to show the distribution.

ggplot(backpack)+
  aes(x = backpack_weight)+
  geom_bar()+
  theme_minimal()+
  labs(
    x = "Backpack weigh (kg)",
    y = "Frequency count",
    title = "Distribution of backpack weights in 100 students")+
  theme(plot.title = element_text(hjust = 0.5))

The graph shows the distribution of backpack weights over 100 students, with a range of weights between 2 and 35kg. TThe most frequent weight is 10kg, and the mean is 11.67kg. he distribution shows a positive skew with the majority of weights are between 4kg and 21 kg. Although the heaviest bag is 35kg, the second heaviest is 25kg.

Q8: Now youve investigated your data separately, create plots to describe the relationship between backpack weight, body weight, gender and reported backpain.

Try both backpack_weight and body_weight as well as the ratio.

Hint: faceting would be useful here.

backpack %>% 
  mutate(back_problems = as.logical(back_problems)) %>%
  ggplot()+
  aes(x = body_weight, y = backpack_weight, colour = back_problems)+
  geom_point(aes(shape = sex), )+
  facet_grid(back_problems ~ sex)+
  theme_light()+
  labs(
    x = "Body weight",
    y = "Backpack weight",
    title = "Relationship between Body and Backpack Weight, Gender and reported Back Pain")+
  theme(plot.title = element_text(hjust = 0.2))

These graphs show a stronger positive relationship between body weight and backpack weight for girls, which is similar for those with or without back pain. The graphs for the boys show far fewer boys have back problems than girls. The boys who dont report back problems show a greater range of body weight and backpack weight than for the girls.

Years 1-5 show overlap in the interquartile range, indicating that there is no significant difference in how heavy backpacks are across these years. However, Year 6 has the lowest median of all years, which is lower than the interquartile boxes for years 2, 3, 4, and 5. All years have maximum outliers but only year 4 has a lower outlier. Overall, there is little difference between Years 1-5 and Year 6 shows the lightest of all the backpacks.
